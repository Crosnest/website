Our first 48 hours

Grateful to everyone who chose to support our validator node from the very beginning, we at Cro's Nest would like to take a moment to check in with you.

Today, the network reached 100 validators, and the number of active users and transactions picked up in pace; we feel it is an excellent time to take a step back and reflect on our launch and our first few hours of operation.

In terms of performance, we are thrilled to be able to say that, starting with Block #35 (just three blocks after we registered to the network), we have processed and signed every single block so far. We helped secure the network proposing some 1% of all blocks since launch.

During these 24 hours, many of you joined us, and a few of you elected to move their stake elsewhere. Having reached and sustained 3 million CRO in stake is a staggering result in such a short time and a strong foundation for future growth.

A word of caution. We have been in touch with several distraught users who had initially chosen to delegate on a given node, then moving to a different one. It is commonly understood that funds are subject to a 28-day lock-up period when releasing them from staking.
What is less commonly discussed is the process and limits of redelegation. Staked coin may be redelegated without any time penalty once, allowing anyone to unconditionally move away from their first choice. Once the stake is transferred to another validator, however, it cannot be redelegated again until the standard 28-day lock-up period has passed. This lock-up is a fundamental mechanism and rule of the underlying blockchain, and neither we nor any other chain operator can remove it.

When you choose to redelegate your funds, please ensure you understand you will be forgoing the right to do so again for 28 days. You will no longer be able to quickly change if your validator suddenly becomes jailed, changes its commercial offering, or encounters performance issues.

Furthering our commitment to the community, we have opened our lobby on Discord, where anyone interested in the Crypto.org Chain or Cro's Nest and its validator is welcome to join. Our team will be happy to support our users and peers, hoping to strengthen the already collaborative and helpful community that has started to grow around this project.

A statement on adjusting validator parameters. We will never change any of our validator's essential parameters (commission rate, minimum self-delegation, moniker, security contact, and website URL) without providing at least three days advanced notice other than in response to governance votes or in case of force-majeure. Notifications will take place both on our website (http://www.cros-nest.com/)  and via e-mail for all registered users.
